Likert scale

On a likert question, respondents specify their level of agreement or disagreement on a symmetric agree-disagree scale for a specific statement. Thus, the range captures the intensity of their feelings for a given question.

An example could be: "How likely are you to listen to this?" with he possible answers very unlikely, unlikely, likely, very likely.

Binary questions

Some questions have only two answer possibilities and therefore classify the answers directly into two often mutually exclusive extremes. This can be useful to generate first tendencies which later can be investigated more granular.

An example could be: "Is building a city the most important step?" with he possible answers yes and no.
